# Color Theory Principles

This skill outlines the methodologies for applying color systems, visual hierarchy, psychological association, and accessibility compliance to design cohesive digital interfaces and brand identities.

## Color Systems and Visual Harmony

### 1. Harmonious Palettes
Utilize established color relationships to construct balanced, intentional palettes:
- **Complementary:** Pair opposite colors on the color wheel to create high contrast and vibrant visual interest.
- **Analogous:** Position adjacent colors together to create serene, comfortable visual flows.
- **Triadic:** Select three evenly spaced colors to maintain high contrast while preserving balance and color richness.

### 2. Palette Scaling and Distribution
- Implement the 60-30-10 rule (60% dominant base tone, 30% secondary structural tone, 10% accent color) to maintain visual hierarchy.
- Establish consistent semantic mapping (e.g., specific colors for success, warning, error, info, and disabled states).

## Psychological and Cultural Associations
- Choose colors that align with the user-experience goals and evoke appropriate emotional responses based on target audience demographics and cultural context.
