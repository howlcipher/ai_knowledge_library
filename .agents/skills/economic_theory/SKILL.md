# Economic Theory Standards

This skill governs the systematic application of macroeconomic and microeconomic principles, market models, and behavioral economics to analyze policy, market trends, and organizational scenarios.

## Core Economic Frameworks

### Microeconomics

- **Supply and Demand**: Analyze price discovery, market equilibrium, price elasticity, and consumer/producer surplus under various market structures.
- **Opportunity Cost**: Evaluate resource allocation options by assessing the net value of the next best alternative foregone.
- **Market Structures**: Distinguish behavior and outcomes across perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, and monopoly. Assess barriers to entry and regulatory impacts.

### Macroeconomics

- **Aggregate Indicators**: Evaluate economic performance using Gross Domestic Product (GDP), inflation rates (CPI/PPI), and unemployment figures.
- **Policy Analysis**: Analyze the impacts of fiscal policy (government spending, taxation) and monetary policy (interest rates, open market operations, reserve requirements) on economic cycles.

### Behavioral Economics

- **Bounded Rationality**: Account for cognitive biases, heuristics, and emotional factors in decision-making models, moving beyond the classical assumption of perfectly rational agents.
- **Nudge Theory**: Design and evaluate decision environments that influence consumer or organizational choices without restricting options.

## Analytical Methodology

- **Empirical Evidence**: Base all economic assertions, forecasts, and impact assessments on empirical datasets, regression analysis, or peer-reviewed literature.
- **Model Validation**: Clearly define the assumptions, constraints, and boundary conditions of any economic model utilized in analysis.
