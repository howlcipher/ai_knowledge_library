# Financial Theory Standards

This skill defines the mathematical frameworks, valuation methodologies, and risk assessment standards required for corporate finance, investment analysis, and financial modeling.

## Core Valuation and Pricing Frameworks

### Discounted Cash Flow

- **Time Value of Money**: Model future cash flows explicitly, adjusting for the time value of money using mathematically rigorous discounting techniques.
- **Cost of Capital**: Calculate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) using the appropriate cost of debt and cost of equity.
- **Cash Flow Projection**: Clearly delineate Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) and Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE). Document all growth assumptions and the terminal value calculation methodology (e.g., Gordon Growth Model or Multiples Approach).

### Capital Asset Pricing Model

- **Expected Returns**: Calculate expected asset returns using the CAPM formula: $E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i (E(R_m) - R_f)$.
- **Parameter Estimation**: Justify the choice of the risk-free rate ($R_f$), the asset's beta ($\beta_i$), and the expected market risk premium ($E(R_m) - R_f$) based on historical or current market data.

### Portfolio Theory

- **Risk-Adjusted Performance**: Evaluate investment portfolios using risk-adjusted metrics such as the Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio, and Treynor Ratio.
- **Diversification**: Model asset covariance and correlation to optimize portfolio asset allocation along the efficient frontier.

## Risk Assessment and Management

- **Quantitative Metrics**: Incorporate quantitative risk measures such as Value at Risk (VaR), Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR), and sensitivity/scenario analysis under stressed market conditions.
- **Risk Classification**: Systematically identify and separate market risk, credit risk, liquidity risk, and operational risk.

## Fiduciary and Professional Standards

- **Mathematical Rigor**: All financial models must be accompanied by explicit assumptions, sensitivity tables, and validation checks to prevent formula errors.
- **Fiduciary Responsibility**: Align all recommendations and models with standard professional ethics, transparency guidelines, and compliance/regulatory mandates.
